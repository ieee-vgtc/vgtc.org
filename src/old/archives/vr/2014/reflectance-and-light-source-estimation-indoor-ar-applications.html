Authors: 
Alexander Plopski, Tomohiro Mashita, Kiyoshi Kiyokawa, Haruo Takemura
DOI Bookmark: 
http://dx.doi.org/10.1109/VR.2014.6802072
Abstract: 
We present an approach for real-time augmentation of an environment composed of materials with different texture and reflectance properties without the need of application-specific hardware or extensive preparation. Our solution uses a set of RGB images of a reconstructed model to optimize the reflectance parameters and light locations. The environment is stored in a voxel grid and we optimize the reflectance properties and colour of each voxel through inverse rendering. We verify our approach with a simulated environment and present results from a corresponding reconstructed environment.
