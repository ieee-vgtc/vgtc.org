Authors: 
Elizabeth Izatt, Kate Scholberg, Regis Kopper
DOI Bookmark: 
http://dx.doi.org/10.1109/VR.2014.6802062
Abstract: 
Water Cherenkov neutrino detectors produce large and multi-layered datasets well-suited to immersive visualization and interaction. We have developed a novel virtual reality (VR) application called Neutrino-KAVE which displays the collocation of photon sensors and their color-coded data within to-scale representations of the Super- and Hyper-Kamiokande neutrino detectors. In this paper, we describe a new visualization technique for neutrino interaction patterns, a mechanism for modifying aspects of the presented data set, and a user interface for system control of this multifaceted application. We also report on its use cases, initial reception, and future development.
