Authors: 
Ana-Despina Tudor, Ilinca Mustatea, Sandra Poeschl, Nicola Doering
DOI Bookmark: 
http://dx.doi.org/10.1109/VR.2014.6802080
Abstract: 
Presentation skills that involve public speaking are an important asset in one???s careers or during one???s study. One way to learn such skills is to use virtual audiences (VA) that simulate the reactions of a live public. A study was conducted to conceptualize the design of such a VA. It was inquired how the nonverbal cues of live audiences vary depending on a speaker???s gaze patterns and vocal loudness. 36 students (listeners) were videotaped during a public speaking situation. The analysis showed that the speaker???s gaze patterns and vocal loudness influenced gaze patterns and body postures of audience members.
