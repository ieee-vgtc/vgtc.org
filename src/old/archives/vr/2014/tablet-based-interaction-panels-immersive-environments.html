Authors: 
David Krum, Thai Phan, Lauren Cairco Dukes, Peter Wang, Mark Bolas
DOI Bookmark: 
http://dx.doi.org/10.1109/VR.2014.6802066
Abstract: 
With the current interest in head mounted displays, we perceived a need for devices that support expressive and adaptive interaction in a low-cost, eyes-free manner. Leveraging rapid prototyping techniques, we have designed and manufactured a variety of panels that can be overlaid on multi-touch tablets and smartphones. The panels are coupled with an app running on the multi-touch device that exchanges commands and state information over a wireless network with the virtual reality application. Sculpted features of the templates provide tactile disambiguation of control widgets and an onscreen head up display provides interaction state information.
