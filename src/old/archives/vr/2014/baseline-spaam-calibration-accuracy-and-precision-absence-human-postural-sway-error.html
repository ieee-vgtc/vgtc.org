Authors: 
Kenneth R. Moser, Magnus Axholt, J. Edward Swan II
DOI Bookmark: 
http://dx.doi.org/10.1109/VR.2014.6802070
Abstract: 
We conducted an experiment to generate baseline accuracy and precision values for optical see-through head mounted display calibration without the inclusion of human postural sway error. This preliminary work will be a control condition for future studies into postural error reduction. SPAAM calibration was performed using 25 alignments taken using the static, sequential, and magic square distance distribution patterns. The accuracy of the calibrations were determined by calculating the extrinsic X, Y, Z translation values from the resulting projection matrix. The results show that the magic square distribution resulted in the most accurate parameter estimation for each extrinsic translation component.
