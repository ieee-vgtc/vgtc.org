Authors: 
Daniela Markov-Vetter, Vanja Zander, Joachim Latsch, Oliver Staadt
DOI Bookmark: 
http://dx.doi.org/10.1109/VR.2014.6802068
Abstract: 
The performance of Augmented Reality (AR) direct object selection coded outside of the human egocentric body frame of reference is decreased under short-term altered gravity. Therefore an adequate countermeasure is required. This paper presents the results of a proof-of-concept study to investigate the impact of simulated hypergravity on target???s size and distance. The study was divided in two experiments, whereby hypergravity was induced by a long-arm human centrifuge and by weights attached to subjects??? dominant arm. The study showed that at higher gravity levels larger target size and larger distance between the targets led to increased performance.
