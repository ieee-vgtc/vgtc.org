Authors: 
Anne Kenyon, John van Rosendale, Samuel Fulcomer, David Laidlaw
DOI Bookmark: 
http://dx.doi.org/10.1109/VR.2014.6802065
Abstract: 
We present the design of Brown University's new Cave, which is expected to be fully operational in Febrary 2014. With 1 arc-minute resolution, 3.8 pi steradians of visual surround, head-tracked stereo, and an almost seamless screen, this Cave offers an upgrade to the state-of-the-art virtual reality experience. We achieve this with, notably, 69 high-resolution long throw projectors, a cylindrical screen with conical ceiling, and a 135 square feet rear-projection floor. Caves have thus far remained impractical for many potential uses due to their limited resolution, brightness, and overall immersion. Brown's new Cave attempts to bridge this gap.
